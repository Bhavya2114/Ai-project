Frequently Asked Questions

General Questions

To claim workplace injury compensation in India, follow these steps: 1) Report the injury to your employer immediately, 2) File a First Information Report (FIR) if necessary, 3) Get medical treatment and maintain records, 4) File a claim under the Employee's Compensation Act, 5) Submit relevant documentation including medical reports and accident details. You may also seek legal representation to help with the claims process.

There are several legal options for resolving family property disputes in India: 1) Mediation through a family counsel, 2) Filing a civil suit in court, 3) Partition suit for ancestral property, 4) Alternative Dispute Resolution (ADR) methods. It's advisable to maintain proper documentation and seek legal counsel to understand your rights and options.

To make a legally valid will in India: 1) The testator must be of sound mind and over 18 years old, 2) The will must be in writing, 3) It should be signed by the testator and two witnesses, 4) Registration is recommended but not mandatory, 5) The will should clearly state the distribution of assets. It's advisable to consult a legal professional for proper drafting and execution.

To report cybercrime in India: 1) File a complaint on www.cybercrime.gov.in, 2) Lodge an FIR at your local police station, 3) Contact the cyber cell of your city police, 4) Preserve all evidence including screenshots and communication records, 5) If it's financial fraud, report to your bank immediately. You can also call the National Cybercrime Reporting Helpline at 1930.

Legal Documents

Adoption in India is governed by specific laws: 1) Register with CARA (Central Adoption Resource Authority), 2) Meet eligibility criteria (age, marital status, etc.), 3) Complete home study report, 4) Wait for child referral, 5) Complete legal documentation and court procedures. The process must comply with the Hindu Adoption and Maintenance Act or the Juvenile Justice Act.

To establish an NGO in India: 1) Choose organization structure (Trust/Society/Section 8 Company), 2) Prepare required documents, 3) Register under appropriate act, 4) Obtain PAN and TAN, 5) Apply for 12A and 80G certificates for tax benefits, 6) FCRA registration if accepting foreign contributions. Consult legal experts for compliance requirements.

To register a company in India: 1) Apply for DSC and DIN, 2) Reserve company name on MCA portal, 3) File incorporation documents (MOA/AOA), 4) Obtain Certificate of Incorporation, 5) Apply for GST, PAN, and other registrations. Ensure compliance with Companies Act 2013, maintain proper records, and file regular returns.

Workplace sexual harassment is governed by the Sexual Harassment of Women at Workplace Act, 2013: 1) Every organization must have an Internal Complaints Committee, 2) Victims can file complaints within 3 months of incident, 3) Employers must provide a safe working environment, 4) Penalties include termination and legal action, 5) Organizations must conduct regular awareness programs and training.
